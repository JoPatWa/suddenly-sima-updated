No Need For Winter To Keep You Down

I

remember growing up reading the American books and the characters there would note how fun it is to be outside sledding on the snow or snow tubing down the hill and making snow angels. From that I longed to experience the snow and moving to the USA my snow dream was realized. However I also learn quickly to fear winter and dread the winter months that I use to love growing up because those months were the hottest in my country.

With the shorter days and arrival of snow and black Ice, the seasonal depression. In my opinion winter has become a synonymous with dread and depression. For some reason this year it has been different, I’ve made a point of practicing gratitude. Here’s 8 ways how to beat the winter blues.

Take a drive

I found to be true that sunrise and sunset are stunning during this time of the year, especially over water. Take a drive to watch the sunrise or the sunset that will sure beat those winter blues away; it doesn’t have to be somewhere special as long as you are being safe.

Be mentally prepared for winter.

To beat winter blues allow to pace yourself and learn to embrace the shorter days. Knowing that they are anomalies and do not last forever. You will get through this.

Join a winter sport

You can join Ice-skating snowboarding, ice fishing, skiing if you join winter sport chances of you experiencing winter blues are zero! You will be busy having fun. Active bodies minds healthy minds.

Find beauty in the winter sunlight

Appreciate the beauty of natural bare trees. There is something so surreal about a snowfall; it has this breath taking serenity.

Go Outside

Spend some time outside. It cold and I know it no fun to be outside during this time but it always better once you actually out and your body will thank you for that vitamin D. take a walk or walk your dog or build a snowman. Still feeling the winter blues?

Take daily supplements

Omega-3 and vitamin D have been linked to the production of serotonin which plays a big role in regulating our mood energy. So take it, I learn this while visiting Czech Republic aka Czechia, people over there takes it like candy and good thing is you can’t overdose on it. These supplements will help combat those winter blues

Drink tea

I grew up drinking more hot tea than coffee, it just one of those traditions that was brought over by English pioneers. So It no coincidence that whenever I’m feeling under weather or sick hot tea is the first thing I go for and it really helps cause of antioxidants, fluorine, caffeine and not to mention nice aroma.

Find strength in your community

This may not work for everyone but if you have a big social circle, you may suggest to your friends a “soup gathering” where all members participate in buying suppliers each gathering and meet once a month and all members will get a chance to host, eat, play board games etc. It’s like a social club and at the same time you develop deeper connection with them and it gives you something to look forward to in your calendar. I also found an article while browsing the net that, Iceland people are one of the happiest people, regardless that they live in snow and they deal with much shorter days. If you have made this far feel free to share how do you combat the darkest of days, while staying warm and mentally healthy
