Beauty Tips - Makeup and Nails

Facial Makeup

W

hen it comes to putting on your face for a night out on the town, a date or a job interview, we can all agree that your makeup should be tidy, fresh and colors be well balanced. When putting a makeup it’s important to prime your face before applying a foundation. This is to prevent your makeup (finished product) appearing “cakey” on your face. Years of wearing foundation has taught me that sometimes when I couldn’t find the exact color match foundation it okay to put a slightly darker tone at least one color below my skin tone. Slightly darker tone foundation gives off that warm radiant look instead of the ashy tacky look when putting a lighter tone on a dark skin tone.

I think the majority of the ladies would agree that having a natural makeup or at least a toned down colors is the way to go for any occasion or a job interview because it gives off a radiant look. As far as makeup products goes to achieve certain appearance, I’ve been bouncing around trying different products from drugstore makeup, NYX, Naked 2, to MAC but on my trip to LA this summer I had forgotten my pallet at home so when I came across Ultra Beauty at the mall I knew what needed to done. I decided to try out another brand; I bought a Lancôme Auda [city] in Paris eye shadow palette to achieve my natural look. So far I’m pleased with the results; I like the fact that the palette contains a mix of matte and sheer colors and it’s honestly the greatest thing for my eyes since makeup remover pads.

As for nails, I keep my nails cherry red. I use Epic Nail products to do my own Shellac. So about 3 times a month I have a DYI day, accompanied by a glass of wine and soothing music and work on my nails.

Nails

As for nails, I keep my nails cherry red. I use Epic Nail products to do my own Shellac. So about 3 times a month I have a DYI day, accompanied by a glass of wine and soothing music and work on my nails. I find it to be the best way to maintain my nails not only for affordability but it also prevents filling nails at the nail salon so flat that whenever they get long they are brittle and chipped. I try my best to keep my nails up to date. Sometimes I would go to the nail salon to get acrylic nails but the reason I don’t do that as often is to minimize filing my nails so flat that I can almost feel my skin under my nail which is not a good feeling. Hopefully you can afford to make sure your nails are never chipped by funding weekly mani’s and pedi’s.

I try to keep my nails in a best condition as possibly as I can but life happens so sometimes I have chipped nail polish. Sue me. I know that my face and nails are not always perfect regardless of my efforts but hubby never complain about my nails or face. Therefore, this denotes that men don’t really care much about intricate nail art, the trendy nail polish color or artistic eye shadow as long as you keep it clean.

I’d love to know any products you ladies use for a natural look or anything that makes you feel and look your best.
