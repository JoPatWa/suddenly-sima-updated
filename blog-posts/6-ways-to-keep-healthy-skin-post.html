6 Tips To Keep Healthy Skin

  • Home
  • Beauty
  • 6 Tips To Keep Healthy Skin
  • Beauty
  • 1 Nov 2017
A

h, September is finally here. September is month that is the doorway to the season that stimulates the soul. Our thoughts drift toward brown sweaters, boots, and hot chocolate. With fall approaching I want to talk about ways to maintain healthy skin because the weather is changing. It is normally dry and cold during the next few months which means you should be taking extra care of your skin. I wanted to describe 6 ways to maintain healthy skin.

Drink Enough Water And Keep A Healthy Diet

We’ve heard this, “you are what you eat” it’s true, the diet you keep is what your skin and body will display on the outside. Also be consistent to your diet because your body will react to what you feed and teach it. Drink enough water but not too much because that may put your health at risk. Drinking way too much water may cause you kidney failure, hallucination and etc. So don’t do it excessively, take what your body can handle. Most physicians would recommend eight-glasses a day as a standard measurement unless somehow you have trained your body to take 2 gallons of water a day, then that’s a different story.

Exfoliate

Remove that dead skin cells to bring up the new glowing and radiant skin. Remember that exfoliation comes in two different methods, one is mechanical and another is chemical. I would advise you to choose the exfoliation method that best suits your skin type, you know what your skin react to and what doesn’t it react to. This is important; especially when you are choosing chemical exfoliation. These products may contain beta hydroxyl acids, alpha and etc. Before you start with the chemical process you may want to visit your dermatologist first. If you are like me and your skin is dry/oily and/or sensitive, you may prefer to use the mechanical exfoliation method that includes a face cloth, or a sponge. Be sure to be gentle because when exfoliation is done wrong it can tear and damage your skin.

Moisturize Your Skin

Moisturize your skin twice a day, in the morning when you wake up and evening before you go to bed. This may differ based on your daily practices but I choose to do it twice a day. During the day we are all busy with our lives, we are in the sun or cold and we need to keep those skin pores closed. I believe that moisturizing your skin twice a day stimulates your skin cells while hydrating your skin and unclogging your pores. You will have to perform this process moderately as overdoing it may cause skin irritation and dry skin

Get Enough Sleep

Get your beauty sleep, your body needs it and your skin will thank you for it. You will wake up feeling refreshed, with a glowing skin and no dark circles underneath your eyes. Studies have been proven that there is correlation between a healthy being and enough sleep, however correlation doesn’t explain causation. So, don’t deprive yourself from a good night sleep.

Take supplements

If you can afford it, you may get the supplements from your pharmacy e.g. Vitamin C or E, Omega-3, Biotin and Iron. These supplements they assist your skin to do what it seems to be lacking. If you’re battling with acne, zinc should be your best friend. It reduces the production of dihydro-testosterone that may be clogging your pores. Take it in moderation as it may cause stomach discomfort.

Wear Sunscreen

Did you know that your skin still get burnt on cooler days as long as the sun is out? Yes, that happens so wear sunscreen before you expose yourself in the sun. Summer is still in and some of us are out in the sun, catching our last summer fun at the beach before we welcome the long cold winter. As fun as it is to be sunbathing at the beach, UVA and UVB rays are our skin’s enemy. UVB is responsible for sunburn and UVA penetrates deep into our skin and ages it. There is a myth that African and African America skin doesn’t get sun-burnt or tanned, not true. Just like the rest of other races African and African American skin does get sun-burnt and tanned, so lather that sunscreen up!
